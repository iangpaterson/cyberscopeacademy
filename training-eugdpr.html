EU GDPR PATHWAY

Effective GDPR Compliance Begins with Professional Training

All organisations in the UK that store and process personal data have until May 2018 to comply with the new EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), or potentially face fines of up to 4% of annual turnover or €20 million. Complying with the new law will require significant updates to policies, processes and service contracts, as well as to the information security measures used to protect this data. To ensure effective compliance, every organisation needs to have managers in place who understand the requirements of the GDPR and are well equipped to plan, implement and maintain a compliance programme. For larger firms there is a mandatory need to appoint a suitably qualified and trained data protection officer (DPO), who will directly report to the highest management level. A recent study by the IAPP indicates that, under the new GDPR, Europe will need at least 28,000 DPO's.

IBITGQ Qualifications

Delegates take the IBITGQ EU General Data Protection Regulation Foundation (EU GDPR F) and/or Practitioner (EU GDPR P) examinations, which consist of 60-minute and 90-minute multiple-choice papers. Qualifications awarded by IBITGQ are accredited to ISO 17024 and are highly regarded by employers throughout the world.

About Cyberscope TV

Cyberscope TV is a crowd sharing platform for opportunities, challenges and technologies changing the world of Cyber Security.

We have secured our on screen comments and chat using an RSA algorithm. Video and audio chats use WebRTC technology which in its turn makes use of DTLS protocol by default. Datagram Transport Layer Security (DTLS) is used to provide communications privacy for datagram protocols. This fosters a secure signaling channel that cannot be tampered with. In other words, no eavesdropping or message forgery can occur on a DTLS encrypted connection.
